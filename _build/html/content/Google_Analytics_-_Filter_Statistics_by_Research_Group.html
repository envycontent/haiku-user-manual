Google Analytics - Filter Statistics by Research Group¶

You can set up filters on Google Analytics so that each Research Group can view statistics for their group but not for the rest of the website.

Research Group web address¶

Go to the Research Group page and make a note of the last part of the web address (in the above example /crg).You will need to know this when you set up your filter.

Go to the Admin section of Google Analytics¶

Log into Google Analytics and click on Admin at the top right hand side of the page.

Click on your site name until you reach the following screen (you might need to click on the name more than once)

Add a New Profile¶

Click on + New Profile

Name your Profile¶

  1. Add a name for your profile (eg the name of the Research Group)
  2. Click on Create Profile

Filters¶

Click on Filters

Add a New Filter¶

Click on + New Filter

Filter properties¶

  1. Enter a name for your filter.
  2. From the drop down boxes select - Include only, traffic to the subdirectories, that contain.
  3. Enter the last part of the Research Group’s web address (see step 1 of this tutorial).
  4. Click on Save.

Create a read only account for the Research Group¶

To create a read only account for the Research Group click on Users

Add user¶

Click on + New User

Enter user details¶

  1. Enter an email address for the Research Group (this address needs to have a google account).
  2. On the Role section ensure that User is selected.
  3. Click on Add User.

When the Owner of the email account logs into Google Analytics they will now be able to see Statistics for pages in their research group but not the rest of the site.
